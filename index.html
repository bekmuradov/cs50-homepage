Hello CS50,
I'm Begench Bekmyradov

I am a self taught web developer. The journey that started with aim of making a living in a less than a year of research and dedication turned into a passion. I didn’t know in the beginning that programming is not just typing a code, but it touches very broad spectrum of ideas, business processes, social issues and many more.

I started programming with Freecodecamp (project I completed while learning here “Tribute page”). Even though I was motivated, but most of the terms and logic behind looked like rocket science at the time. After a month of studying I stumbled into Codecademy. But my experience was the same as with Freecodecamp.

Around the same time I started reading a book “Learning Web Design” by Jennifer Robbins and book series “You don’t know JS” by Kyle Simpson. In the next three month I completed Galvanize Software Engineering Free Prep Program and did some studying on MDN. By this time I already could do “Image Gallery”, “Guess the Number” and “Bouncing Ball”. Finally I could see some results which doubled my motivation. I continued studying by watching Youtube video tutorials from “Traversy Media” and “The Coding Train”, which I still follow. It took a bit longer than I expected, because at the same time I worked full time in a completely different setting.

With the start of lockdown due to COVID 19 I could dedicate all my time and attention to programming. I took CS50 Open Course Ware. I completed 8 weeks taught by David J. Malan. This course gave me confidence and summarized all my previous knowledge. I feel like jigsaw in my head completed and everything in programming started making sense. Now, I understand the logic behind the code that I write and other codes I see.

If I were to go through this journey of learning one more time I would do it in the order bellow:

Phase A | fundamental knowledge

  1. CS50 Open Course Ware (will give you some understanding, logic behind code and motivation)

Phase B | learn by doing platforms – way of getting your hands in coding

  1. “Learning Web Design” 5th edition book by Jennifer Robbins (basic knowledge on HTML, CSS and Javascript)
  2. MDN Learn Web Development and their documentation (probably you will read for the rest of your life)
  3. Freecodecamp
  4. Codecademy

Phase C | learn by doing platforms – way of getting your hands in coding (advanced)

  1. “You don’t know JS” 2nd edition by Kyle Simpson (you’ll get more complete understanding of Javascript)
  2. Galvanize Software Engineering Free Prep Program
  3. Youtube video tutorials “Traversy Media” and “The Coding Train”

I appreciate all the work done by developers of this courses and platforms. It’s amazing to have a chance to learn programming in the comfort of your home.
